Section 01 — DTCH Team Chat

Space Restructure

Reorganize chat spaces around actual dispensary workflows. Kill dead spaces, add mission-critical ones, rename jargon to operator language.

1

Restructure Spaces for Real Workflows Critical

All 4 agents agreed: current spaces don't match how dispensary teams actually work

The current 8 spaces mix abstract concepts ("Growth Lab", "Pulse") with operational needs. Budtenders told the Ops agent they only use 3-4 spaces max. Restructure into clear operational zones:

  • Keep & Rename: Stock Room → Inventory, Floor Ops → Sales Floor, Guest XP → Customer Issues
  • Add: 💵 Cash Desk (cash mgmt, safe counts, armored car), 📋 Compliance (METRC, audits, regulatory), 🏪 Manager's Office (private ops, P&L, staffing), 🤝 Vendor Alley (supplier comms, purchase orders)
  • Kill: Watercooler (no one uses social chat at work), merge Bridge Feed + Pulse → single "AI Alerts" space
  • Add MSO support: Location scoping — filter all spaces by store for multi-location operators

Section 02 — DTCH Team Chat

Tri-State Panel Layout

Replace the current full-screen modal with a three-state panel: collapsed icon rail, sidebar, and full-width view. Users stay in context while chatting.

2

Three-State Progressive Disclosure Critical

Design agent primary recommendation — backed by Ops "nobody wants a full-screen takeover" feedback

The current DTCH panel is a full-screen modal overlay that completely removes the user from their workflow. Budtenders on the floor need to glance at messages while working in POS. Replace with a right-anchored panel that has three states:

  • Rail (48px): Icon strip showing space icons with unread badges. Always visible, zero context-switch cost.
  • Sidebar (320px): Click a rail icon to expand. Shows messages in current space. Click again or ESC to collapse back to rail.
  • Full (640px+): Double-click or drag to expand fully. Shows message list + detail panel side-by-side. For deep investigation.

Command Palette & Actions

Replace slash commands with a searchable command palette (⌘K) that surfaces contextual actions based on the active space.

3

Contextual Command Palette High

Design agent: "Slash commands are a power-user tax. 90% of operators don't know they exist."

Current slash commands (/inventory, /sentiment, /report, etc.) require memorization. Replace with a unified ⌘K command palette that:

  • Shows context-aware actions based on active space (Inventory shows reorder/audit, Cash Desk shows safe count/reconcile)
  • Fuzzy search across all commands, spaces, team members, and recent conversations
  • Recent actions section for quick re-use
  • Keyboard-first but also accessible via the toolbar button

Section 04 — DTCH Team Chat

Layered Card System

Replace walls of text with progressive-disclosure cards: collapsed summary → expanded detail → full panel.

4

Three-Layer Information Cards High

Design + Ops consensus: "AI Bridge dumps too much info. Budtenders need the headline, not the essay."

When Bridge AI surfaces a report, alert, or data card, show it in progressive layers:

  • Layer 1 — Collapsed (1 line): Icon + headline + key metric + timestamp. "📦 Low Stock Alert: Guru Concentrates — 4 units left"
  • Layer 2 — Expanded (in-chat): Click to expand within the message. Shows data table, chart, or action buttons. No navigation away.
  • Layer 3 — Detail Panel: Click "View full" to open a side panel with complete data, history, and action options.

Alert Tiers & Shift Handoff

Three-tier alert system replaces the single notification bell. Shift handoff protocol ensures nothing falls through the cracks at shift change.

5

Three-Tier Alert System High

Ops agent: "A reorder notice and a METRC audit alert should NOT look the same"

  • ■ CRITICAL — Red banner, sound alert, persists until acknowledged. POS down, compliance violation, cash discrepancy, METRC sync failure.
  • ■ ACTION — Amber badge, no sound, dismissible. Low stock reorder, shift handoff pending, customer complaint escalation.
  • ■ INFO — Subtle inline, auto-dismiss after read. Daily sales summary, sentiment trend update, team member joined.
Ops Design Finance
6

Shift Handoff Protocol High

PMM + Ops: "Biggest operational gap — things get dropped between shifts"

Add a /handoff command or ⌘K action that generates an AI-powered shift summary:

  • Open issues from current shift (unresolved customer complaints, pending reorders)
  • Cash status (current safe balance, variance from expected)
  • Inventory alerts triggered during shift
  • Key metrics vs goals (sales pacing, traffic, basket size)
  • Free-text notes from outgoing shift lead

Section 06 — DTCH Team Chat

POS & Compliance Integrations

Real-time POS transaction triggers and METRC compliance integration surface operational events directly in DTCH spaces.

7

POS Real-Time Transaction Triggers Medium

Finance + Ops: "DTCH should know about big sales, voids, and discount abuse as they happen"

  • Large Order Alert: Transaction > $500 → Cash Desk + Manager's Office
  • Void/Return Alert: Any void > $50 or return → Manager's Office for review
  • Discount Threshold: Discount > 25% or cumulative shift discounts > $500 → Manager's Office
  • Speed Alert: Transaction time > 8 min → Sales Floor (may need backup)
Finance Ops
8

METRC & Compliance Integration High

Ops agent: "Compliance is the #1 existential risk. It should be front and center."

  • Harvest Ready: METRC harvest batch ready for processing → Compliance space
  • Manifest Due: Transfer manifest needs filing within 24h → Critical alert
  • Inventory Reconciliation: METRC vs POS variance detected → Compliance space + Manager
  • License Renewal: 30/60/90 day reminders for license expiration
  • Audit Mode: One-click "audit prep" that generates required METRC reports

Section 07 — Customer Portal

Account Health Dashboard

Replace the current ticket-first landing page with an Account Health Dashboard that immediately shows operators what matters: their health score, ROI, and opportunity gaps.

9

Account Health Score (0-100) Critical

All 4 agents: "First thing an operator wants to know — am I in good shape?"

A composite health score replacing the current "Open Tickets" KPI row. Components:

  • POS Uptime (25% weight) — system availability over 30 days
  • Ecommerce Conversion (25%) — online menu → checkout completion rate
  • Payment Volume (25%) — transactions processed vs capacity
  • Support Velocity (25%) — open ticket age and resolution time
PMM Finance Ops Design
10

ROI Summary Card Critical

Finance agent: "Show them exactly how much Dutchie makes them vs what they pay"

A persistent card showing the operator's return on their Dutchie subscription:

  • Revenue Influenced: Total revenue attributed to Dutchie channels (ecommerce, kiosk, etc.)
  • ROI Multiplier: Revenue ÷ Subscription Cost (e.g., "15.9x return")
  • Time Saved: Hours saved on compliance, inventory, ordering automation
  • Benchmark: "Top 25% of operators your size see 22x ROI — here's how to close the gap"

Section 08 — Customer Portal

AI-First Support Experience

Replace the ticket-first support model with search-first + KB + smart escalation. Most operators want to self-serve.

11

Search → KB → Escalation Flow Critical

Ops agent: "Operators don't want to file a ticket. They want an answer NOW."

Redesign the support tab around instant resolution:

  • Search bar front and center: "How do I set up loyalty points?" → instant KB article results
  • AI-powered answers: If no exact match, Bridge AI synthesizes an answer from KB + past tickets
  • Smart escalation: Only after search + AI fails, offer ticket creation with pre-filled context from the search
  • Integration status dashboard: POS online/offline, API status, sync lag — the #1 reason operators contact support
Ops Design PMM
12

Integration Health Dashboard Critical

Ops agent: "The #1 thing operators want in a portal is: IS MY STUFF WORKING?"

  • POS Status: Online/offline per register, uptime %, last heartbeat
  • Ecommerce Sync: Menu sync status, last successful sync, items out of sync
  • Payment Processing: Terminal status, transaction success rate, batch close status
  • METRC Connection: Sync lag, last successful report, pending transfers

Product Adoption & Benchmarking

Show operators which features they're using (and which they're leaving on the table), with peer benchmarking to drive adoption.

13

Feature Utilization Dashboard High

Finance: "Most operators pay for features they don't use. Show them."

  • Visual grid of all Dutchie features with utilization % (0-100%)
  • Color-coded: Green (active), Amber (underutilized), Gray (not activated)
  • Click any feature → setup guide, training video, or "Turn on" button
  • Monthly progress tracker showing adoption improvement
Finance PMM
14

Peer Benchmarking with Opportunity Gap High

Finance agent's detailed model: "Stores like yours see +23% repeat rate with loyalty"

  • Cohort: Compare against "stores like yours" (same state, similar revenue, similar product mix)
  • Metrics: Ecommerce conversion, avg basket, loyalty enrollment, repeat rate, online order %
  • Opportunity Gap: "You're leaving $155K/year on the table vs top-quartile peers"
  • Action items: Specific recommendations ranked by estimated revenue impact

Billing Intelligence

Transform the billing tab from a static invoice list into intelligent cost analysis with utilization insights and 280E optimization.

16

Usage-Based Billing Nudges Medium

Finance: "Show operators what they're paying for vs what they're using"

  • Each line item shows utilization % alongside cost
  • Underutilized features highlighted with "You're paying $X/mo for this but only using Y%"
  • Right-size recommendations: "Switch to Tier 1 and save $200/mo" or "Upgrade — you've outgrown this tier"

New Tab Architecture: HOME / SUPPORT / ACCOUNT / PRODUCT Critical

Design agent: "Three tabs aren't enough. Four tabs map to operator mental models."

  • HOME: Health dashboard, ROI card, opportunity gap, quick actions
  • SUPPORT: Search + KB + AI + integration health + tickets
  • ACCOUNT: Billing, invoices, usage analytics, 280E report, subscription management
